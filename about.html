Final Fantasy 14, known commonly as FFXIV, is a subscription-based Massively Multiplayer Online Role Playing Game (MMORPG) based on the lore and style of the Final Fantasy series. In the game, players can play as a variety of Jobs from different Roles (Tank, DPS, Healer), each with its unique playstyle and skillset. The game boasts an extensive story, and each patch updates the game with new Main Story Quests (MSQ). Through the MSQs, the game feels reminiscent of a traditional JRPG game.

In addition to MSQs, there are other means to enjoy the game. Interacting with fellow players throughout the expansive world, defeating open-world bosses, and the Casino are some ways players spend their time. Arguably one of the most popular game contents is the game's endgame content: Savage and Ultimate raids. These contents require high skill and coordinated teamwork, involving intricate puzzle-like mechanics in difficult boss battles. Both Savage and Ultimate raids are challenged with 8-man parties. Savage raids are considered difficult endgame content, and Ultimate raids are considered the most difficult, challenging battles available in the game.



A table describing each job can be found below, and more information can be found on the official FFXIV Jobs page. Jobs can be unlocked as the player pleases; players simply have to complete a small quest to unlock the prerequisite class, and at Level 30, can unlock the corresponding job. From then, players can complete content to level up the Job.
In addition to the three main roles (Tank, Healer, DPS), there are subroles within the DPS role: Melee, Physical Ranged, and Caster.

Melee DPS generally have no cast-time associated with their skills, but must remain close to the enemy (in melee range). In exchange, Melee Jobs are capable of high damage.
Physical Ranged Jobs can attack from a distance with no or few cast-times on their skills. Their range allows them to move and dodge enemy attacks more easily, in exchange for dealing less damage.
Casters are also ranged, but have cast-time associated with their skills; while a skill is being casted, the player cannot move, or the skill will be canceled. Though they are the least mobile, Casters are caoable of high damage in exchange. Note: Scholar and Summoner stem from the same base class, so when a player reaches Level 30 Arcanist, they are able to unlock both Scholar and Summoner. In addition, because they share the same class, the two jobs share experience points (EXP) (if a player is gaining EXP on Summoner, the EXP will apply to Scholar as well).

Paladin (PLD) Tank Like a knight from a fairy tale, Paladins use their sword and shield to protect their allies. They can use holy magic to damage enemies or heal their party members.
Warrior (WAR) Tank High damage, high healing. Warriors use a Greataxe and have numerous self-healing abilities, deal decent damage, and pull in mobs of enemies efficiently.
Dark Knight (DRK) Tank Acknowledging the darkness within oneself, Dark Knights use this energy to slay foes and protect their allies from further danger. Also wields a cool Greatsword.
Gunbreaker (GNB) Tank A fast-paced Tank with lots of skill clicking that DPS Job enjoyers would find familiar. Their Gunblades hold enemy attention while their allies wear them down.
White Mage (WHM) Healer Drawing from nature, White Mages provide pure, potent healing and use earthen magicks to aid their allies in battle.
Scholar (SCH) Healer Don't want to manually heal? Scholars have a helpful fairy that helps automatically heal party members! Using their intellect, Scholars protect their allies with shields while wearing down enemy defenses.
Astrologian (AST) Healer Using the heart of the cards, Astrologians provide damaging buffs to their allies while providing regenerative healing.
Sage (SGE) Healer Sages have a flexible and engaging skillset, utilizing knowledge and medicine to strike enemies and provide shields to allies.
Dragoon (DRG) DPS/Melee Who doesn't want to dramatically jump and strike with a super cool lance? Consistent damage dealers with LOTS of jumping abilities.
Monk (MNK) DPS/Melee Monks have fast-paced playstyle that requires you to be able to think fast. They boast high damage and possibly the strongest fists on this list.
Ninja (NIN) DPS/Melee Ninjas can unleash powerful Ninjutsu spells from afar, then close the distance with deadly strikes of their knives. Difficult to learn, rewarding to play.
Samurai (SAM) DPS/Melee Wielding a Katana, Samurais are selfish, heavy damage dealers with a cyclic skill rotation (my personal favorite).
Reaper (RPR) DPS/Melee With a rhythmic skill combo, Reapers deal heavy damage with their Scythes and phantom buddies.
Bard (BRD) DPS/Physical Ranged Bards provide buffs to their allies through their songs, and chip away at enemy health with potent poisons and windshears.
Machinist (MCH) DPS/Physical Ranged The most damage-focused of Physical Ranged Jobs, Machinist boast a fast-paced playstyle paired with big damage.
Dancer (DNC) DPS/Physical Ranged Dancers offer valuable buffs to heal, protect, and strengthen their allies.
Black Mage (BLM) DPS/Caster Wielding destructive Black magic, Black mages offer high, selfish damage through various spells of thunder, fire, and ice.
Summoner (SMN) DPS/Caster Summoners call upon powerful Gods and Beasts to fight alongside them in battle, from Bahamut to cute Carbuncles. They also cast powerful spells that deal damage over time.
Red Mage (RDM) DPS/Caster Balancing both Black and White magic, Red Mages boast both utility, damage, and mobility through their ability to insta-cast their spells.
